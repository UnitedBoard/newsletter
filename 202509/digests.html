The 2025 Cohort of Minders and Carers

 

The Asian Academy for Campus Ministry (AACM) 2025, held at Hong Kong Baptist University from April 30 to May 6, brought together 30 educators and campus ministers from 12 countries across Asia. Under the theme “Nurturing Minds, Empowering Lives: Advancing Mental Health and Mentorship in Campus Communities”, the academy focused on equipping participants with new perspectives and practical strategies to support student well-being and holistic development throughout their college education and beyond.

 

Throughout the week, the participants immersed themselves in keynote reflections, collaborative workshops, and experiential learning. A highlight was the drama therapy session, which proved transformative for many. Inchol Yang, professor and chaplain at Yonsei University, South Korea, was deeply moved by the experience, observing powerful emotional breakthroughs among participants.

Soth Sok, dean of education at the Royal University of Phnom Penh (RUPP), Cambodia, was equally impressed by the session on youth mental health strategies which provided a sobering look at the realities faced by students today.

 

Litty Joseph, assistant professor of commerce and campus minister of St. Joseph’s University in Bengaluru, India, reflects, "AACM 2025 was not just a professional development opportunity, but a powerful reminder that campus ministers across Asia share similar challenges and aspirations."

 

The academy fostered a vibrant sense of community and cross-border collaboration. Participants shared best practices, developed actionable plans for their home institutions, and engaged in cultural immersion activities across Hong Kong.

AACM 2025 concluded with participants presenting their action plans, demonstrating a strong commitment to implementing new mental health and mentorship initiatives across the region. With renewed inspiration, practical tools, and strengthened networks, AACM participants are poised to create lasting ripple effects, nurturing resilience and compassion on campuses throughout Asia.

 

Summer in Singapore Welcomes a New Cohort of United Board Fellows

The United Board Summer Leadership Institute 2025 at Singapore Management University (SMU) proved to be an inspiring and transformative experience for the 25 Fellows from eight Asian countries. For five days from July 7, the program fostered deep discussion, hands-on learning, and powerful peer connections around the future of higher education in Asia.

 

The Institute provided a powerful platform for collaboration, personal growth, and strategic insight. The cohort of 2025-26 explored leadership through diverse lenses—strategic planning, persuasion, and fundraising. The program fostered lifelong networks, equipping the Fellows to champion excellence and innovation at their institutions. Among others, Prof. Roy Chua guided them through the themes of influence and persuasion, using the film 12 Angry Men to anchor discussions.

The Fellows immersed themselves in sessions that challenged them to rethink university leadership, explore strategies for excellence and innovation, and engage with current trends such as internationalization, entrepreneurship, and digital transformation. Through group discussions, institutional visits, and reflection, they examined the opportunities and complexities of leading universities in a rapidly changing world.

 

Camaraderie and shared purpose marked each day—from animated debates on strategic planning and university ranking, to practical workshops around fundraising and centering leadership. They also had time to tour SMU’s innovative campus centers—including the Career Centre, Student Wellness, and the Business Incubator—gaining hands-on insights into student empowerment and social innovation.

Dr. David Johns, a seasoned academic and university leader who joined the program as external assessor, observed: “The Fellows Program serves as a bridge linking institutions, cultures, and individuals to a larger vision of Asian higher education.” The Institute closed with Dr. Johns’s reflections on “Leading from the Center”, encouraging the Fellows to embrace complexity, humility, and gratitude.

 

 

Integrating AI for a Robust Future in Christian Higher Education

The United Board recently launched a five-part Alumni Webinar Series to resounding success. The inaugural session—"Integrating AI to Build a Robust Future for Christian Higher Education in Asia”—held on July 29, 2025, drew over 240 from across the region who gathered virtually to explore how artificial intelligence can empower educators, learners, and leaders in Asian higher education.

 

Mr. Christian Aung, Education Technology Officer, led a dynamic discussion on closing the skills gap between traditional education and the rapidly evolving AI-driven job market, focusing on themes such as AI-readiness in higher education, ethical leadership in the age of AI, and the changing role of educators.

 

Christian discussed the widening gap between academic offerings and technological development. He then demonstrated various transformative AI tools that are revolutionizing both educational and professional sectors, such as AI agents capable of automating complex tasks, multimodal large language models (LLMs) that process and reason with texts, images, and audios, as well as tools for 3D modeling and simulation used to create interactive learning environments.

 

Participant feedback was overwhelmingly positive, with many praising the session’s organization, engaging content, and relevance to their professional growth. We look forward to building on this momentum in the upcoming sessions, continuing to support our educational leaders in shaping a future-ready, values-driven higher education experience.

 

Virtual Mentorship Training Asia 2025

The United Board recently organized the Virtual Mentorship Training Asia 2025, a dynamic five-week program specially designed for educators across the region. Topics focused on what effective mentoring looks like; the role of effective communication in mentor-mentee relationships; navigating difficult situations; conflict management and fostering independence; cultivating ethical behaviours; fostering mentee independence; and AI. This year’s cohort brought together 32 educators from 19 higher education institutions in seven Asian countries, reflecting a diverse and inclusive learning environment.

 

Participants were actively engaged in different interactive exercises, analysing case studies, small and large group discussions, and developing their own personal mentorship philosophy. Gina Fontejon Bonior, dean of Silliman University’s College of Education, says, “This course not only provided me a framework on mentoring but also immersed me in the experiences of my coursemates and mentors, in ways that I could not get in asynchronous micro-credential courses.”

 

She is echoed by Jayasundari of St. Christopher’s College of Education: “This training journey so far has been both enriching and thought-provoking. It has given me the opportunity to realign my mentoring approach with deeper clarity and purpose.”

 

Congratulations to all the educators who completed this rewarding program, underscoring the United Board’s commitment to professional development and innovative teaching practices in Asia!

 

Strengthening Whole Person Education in Northeast India

The Faculty Development Program on "Faculty as Whole Person Educators", held during August 11–15, 2025 in Dimapur, Nagaland, exemplified the United Board's ongoing commitment to advancing Whole Person Education in Northeast India. In partnership with Don Bosco College, Maram, the program empowered faculty to integrate service-learning, competence-based education, technology, and research for social good into their teaching practices.

 

Faculty participants engaged with key educational themes—curriculum mapping, psychology of learning, and institutional visioning—while reflection and mindfulness sessions fostered personal and professional growth. The event marked the second United Board-led initiative in the region, further strengthening institutional leadership and transformative teaching in Asia.

 

Special appreciation is extended to Whole Person Education Academy alumni—Dr. Vijay Solomon, Dr. Samrat, Dr. Nithila Devakarunyam, and Dr. Alphy Geever—who returned as facilitators to share their expertise and inspire the participants. This program established a vibrant community of educators dedicated to Whole Person Education and will continue to inspire transformative practices in Northeast India long after its conclusion.
