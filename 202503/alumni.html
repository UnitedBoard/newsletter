United Board Alumni Gather and Grow

Chennai reunion reignites alumni network potential

1 February marked a milestone not only in reconnecting with alumni but also in renewing efforts to build a self-sustaining alumni community. At Women’s Christian College, Chennai, 38 United Board alumni—ranging from the earliest Fellows Program cohorts to recent Whole Person Education Academy graduates—gathered to celebrate their shared journey, reconnect, and explore ways to deepen their collective impact.

After a warm welcome by Dr. Pareena Gupta Lawrence, United Board President, participants engaged in focussed group discussions. In the Fellows group, alumni reflected on how their experience with the United Board Fellows program had shaped their personal and professional growth. One participant suggested compiling a set of “Most Significant Change” stories, that capture transformative moments—to serve as a resource and inspiration for the broader community.

In the faculty development group, alumni shared practical insights on how they had applied what they had learned—improving their teaching practices, mentoring colleagues, and advocating for institutional change.

The Chennai gathering affirmed a powerful truth: the United Board’s impact is multiplied through its alumni. As participants departed, they carried not only fond memories but also renewed sense of purpose in shaping the future of higher education in Asia.

Sustaining Leadership Growth with Joseph Zolner

The first instalment in the United Board Alumni Webinar Series was held on 17 March (US)/18 March (Asia) featuring Dr. Joseph Zolner, United Board Trustee and Adjunct Lecturer of Education at the Harvard Graduate School of Education. The session, titled “Sustaining Your Leadership Growth", was very well received.

We were delighted to welcome more than 100 alumni from across Asia, representing institutions in India, Indonesia, the Philippines, Cambodia, Myanmar, Vietnam, and beyond. The audience included both recent program participants and seasoned alumni, with roles ranging from senior administrators to faculty members.

Drawing on his extensive experience in leadership development and higher education curricular innovation, Dr. Zolner offered a deeply personal and practical perspective on effective leadership. He emphasized that leadership is “context-dependent, requiring adaptability to who you are, where you are, and how you act in specific situations.” Further, he encouraged participants to focus on implementation, noting that effective leadership is rooted in continuous personal growth and self-reflection. Success lies in the ability to identify one’s strengths, weaknesses, and blind spots, and to turn insights into actionable outcomes.
